I am Ku Tian, a second-year Ph.D. student at Hefei University of Technology. My research interests mainly focus on satellite mission planning, with particular attention to related theories, methods, and practical applications. I am currently conducting research in the Aerospace System Management Laboratory under the supervision of Prof. Xiaoxuan Hu. My academic work is driven by a strong interest in addressing challenging problems in space mission organization and optimization. Through my research, I hope to contribute to the development of efficient and intelligent approaches for satellite task scheduling and mission planning. I am also enthusiastic about exploring new ideas, collaborating with others, and applying theoretical methods to real-world aerospace systems.

Personal experience
======
I have been fortunate to participate in a number of research projects under the guidance of my advisor, which has greatly broadened my academic perspective and research experience. Through these projects, I have developed a deeper understanding of on-orbit satellite mission planning and gained solid knowledge of the mission operation and control process. In particular, I have become familiar with several important aspects of this field, including orbit propagation, access calculation, and related operational analysis. These experiences have not only strengthened my technical background, but also helped me build a more systematic understanding of satellite mission planning in practical applications.

Personal hobbies

Beyond academic research, I have a wide range of personal interests and greatly enjoy participating in sports. In particular, I am fond of ball games such as table tennis, badminton, and basketball, which not only help me stay active and energetic, but also allow me to develop teamwork and perseverance.
